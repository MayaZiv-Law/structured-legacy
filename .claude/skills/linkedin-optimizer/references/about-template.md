# About Section Template

Fill in the placeholders. Keep the structure, cut anything that doesn't serve the reader.

**Budget:** 2,600 chars total. First ~220 chars (Hook + first sentence) must earn the "...see more" click.

---

## Template

```
[HOOK — one line. A stat, a contrarian take, or a sharp problem. No "I'm a passionate..."]

[WHAT YOU DO + WHO FOR — one sentence. "I help [audience] do [outcome] through [method]."]

[TRANSITION LINE — optional, sets up the proof.]

What I've done:
• [PROOF POINT 1 — metric + scope. e.g., "Scaled organic traffic 12x at [Company] in 18 months."]
• [PROOF POINT 2 — named logo or outcome. e.g., "Led the GTM for [Product], cited in TechCrunch and The Information."]
• [PROOF POINT 3 — financial or pipeline outcome. e.g., "Sourced $14M in marketing-attributed pipeline in FY24."]
• [PROOF POINT 4 — optional, awards / speaking / press.]

How I work:
[2-4 sentences on your POV. What do you believe about marketing that most people don't? What's your unfair advantage? This is the section that makes you memorable — don't skip it.]

What I'm focused on now:
[1-2 sentences on the current role/project, or what you're looking for next. Keep it forward-looking.]

Let's talk if:
• [You're a [ROLE] at a [COMPANY STAGE/TYPE] company looking for [OUTCOME]]
• [You want to [SPECIFIC USE CASE]]
• [You're hiring for [ROLE] and want to see work samples]

[CTA — email, Calendly link, or "DM me". Pick one. Make it easy.]
```

---

## Commentary

- **The Hook is the whole game.** If it doesn't stop the scroll, the rest doesn't matter. Write 5 options, pick the one that would embarrass you slightly — that's usually the honest one.
- **"I help [audience] do [outcome]"** is the clearest second line because it's the question every viewer is silently asking: *what do you do and should I care?*
- **Bullets beat paragraphs** for proof. The eye finds them. Use real numbers or don't use numbers at all — vague ones ("significant growth") read as a lie.
- **"How I work"** is where most marketers quit. Don't. This is the section that converts viewers into conversations because it's the only section that couldn't be written about anyone else.
- **"Let's talk if"** is a reverse-qualifier — it tells the wrong audience to leave and the right audience they're home. More effective than "Open to opportunities."
- **One CTA, not three.** If you list your email, your Calendly, and your website, people pick none. Pick the one you'll actually respond to.
- **Avoid:** third-person bios ("Jane is a marketing leader..."), mission-statement language ("on a mission to..."), lists of skills (that's what the Skills section is for), and any sentence with the word "passionate".
