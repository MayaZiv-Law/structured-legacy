# Banner Brief — Maya Ziv Law

_LinkedIn banner spec: **1584 × 396 px**, under 8 MB. JPG/PNG. The left third is partially covered by your profile photo on desktop — keep copy in the right two-thirds._

_Goal: **more visibility → more leads.** Banner is the biggest real estate on your profile — it must communicate value prop + trust in under 2 seconds._

---

## Design goals

1. Communicate your value prop in **≤ 5 words**.
2. Reinforce the finance + law + cross-border positioning.
3. Match your existing MZ Law logo / visual identity (keep typography + palette consistent with the mark already shown in your Experience section).
4. Avoid legal-industry clichés — they flatten your differentiation.

---

## Recommended direction (A — "The Bridge")

**Tagline (pick one, test):**
- "Where law, finance, and business meet."
- "Transactional counsel for complex deals."
- "Legal strategy, with a finance accent."
- "US – Israel. Complex deals. Done." (most punchy, most founder-friendly)

**Visual:**
- Minimalist. Solid or subtle gradient background (navy/charcoal → warm off-white).
- Thin line-art motif suggesting a bridge or two skylines (NY + TLV) — geometric, not literal.
- Tagline in right 2/3 in a clean serif (Playfair, Lora, or your firm's brand serif).
- Bottom-right: small wordmark **MAYA ZIV LAW** + optional contact (email or website).

**Palette:**
- Primary: deep navy `#0E2A47` (trust, finance)
- Accent: warm gold `#C9A961` or sage `#7A8B76` (matches your existing MZ logo mark)
- Neutral: off-white `#F6F3EC`

---

## Alternative direction (B — "The Document")

More traditional-legal, but elevated.

**Visual:**
- Off-white or cream background.
- Subtle typewriter-style line of contract text, partially visible, fading to the right (art-directed blur).
- Over it, tagline in a modern sans-serif (Inter, Söhne).
- Bottom-right: monogram `MZ` in gold foil finish.

**Pros:** signals "serious transactional lawyer" fast.
**Cons:** slightly more conventional — less differentiated.

---

## Alternative direction (C — "The Data Point")

Leans hardest into the finance credential — good if your ICP is fintech founders and VCs.

**Visual:**
- Background: dark charcoal.
- One elegant data-visual motif on the right (minimal line chart or ticker-tape style).
- Left tagline: _"Law that understands the balance sheet."_
- Bottom-right: MZ Law wordmark in off-white.

**Pros:** memorable, strong differentiation.
**Cons:** narrows ICP perception to finance/fintech — if you also want real-estate and general M&A inbound, use A instead.

---

## Do NOT use

- Gavel, scales of justice, law-book stacks
- Stock handshake photography
- Courtroom columns / marble
- Generic "Law Office of..." lockup in serif
- Color-blocked LinkedIn-template designs (the "Canva default" look signals no taste)
- Quote from a dead philosopher

---

## Production

**Easiest path:** recreate Direction A in Canva.
1. Create a 1584×396 design.
2. Background: `#0E2A47`.
3. Add a thin horizontal gold line across the middle-right.
4. Tagline in serif, 52–64 px, off-white.
5. Wordmark bottom-right, 18–22 px, gold.
6. Export as PNG at 100% quality.
7. Upload to LinkedIn → check on mobile + desktop (profile photo covers part of the bottom-left on desktop only).

**Budget option to hire out:** $80–150 on Fiverr ("LinkedIn banner designer" — filter for lawyers / professional services examples). Give them this brief verbatim.

**Premium option:** if you want the banner + logo + website hero to share visual language, brief a small brand designer (~$800–1,500) for a mini-identity pack. Only worth it if the firm is client-facing long-term.
