## What to DROP from your current profile

If any of these are on your profile, remove or deprioritize them — they dilute search relevance:

- Generic soft skills alone ("Teamwork," "Communication," "Leadership") — keep only if paired with a practice-area skill.
- "Microsoft Word" / "Outlook" — too generic; wastes a slot.
- Marketing-specialist-era skills that don't translate to legal (unless intentionally positioning as commercially-experienced — then keep 1–2 like "Vendor Management" or "Commercial Operations").

## Endorsement strategy (to lock in your top 3)

LinkedIn shows the skills with the **most endorsements** most prominently. To secure your pinned three:

1. Ask 5–10 colleagues (ex-Citi, Heskia-Hacmun, Vornado peers) to endorse specifically **M&A**, **Cross-Border Transactions**, and **Commercial Contracts** — not random skills.
2. Reciprocate: endorse their top-3 skills back, same week.
3. After 10+ endorsements each, the top-3 pin becomes near-impossible to dislodge algorithmically.

**Do not** use LinkedIn's "endorse 5 random skills" bulk prompt on others — it inflates weak skills and waters down yours.

## Custom / niche skill terms to consider adding

LinkedIn's skill taxonomy accepts custom terms (type and press enter). Useful for niche search:
- "US–Israel Cross-Border Law"
- "Transactional Due Diligence"
- "Startup Legal Counsel"
- "Deal Negotiation"

Custom skills rank lower in endorsement weight but still show up in exact-match search — worth 1–2 slots.
